"Shoot me instead of him." Kneeling Myanmar nun.



According to the Guardian on the 9th (local time), Sister An Rose Nuthaung (nun Rose), a member of St. Francis Saberio's convent
in Michini City, Myanmar, knelt in front of heavily armed military police and begged them not to take their children's lives. "I knelt
down and begged her not to shoot or torture the children, but rather kill me," Rose told the newspaper.

Myanmar's military has been cracking down on civilians, including live ammunition and tear gas, after a February 1 coup. Citizens
took to the streets with helmets and homemade shields in Michini, northern Myanmar. Myanmar's military and police began to
flock around the protesters to suppress them.

Rose and two other nuns begged to go to the police and go somewhere else. Some military and police officers were seen listening
to the nun when Sister Rose knelt down, kneeling down and listened to the nun.

But while Rose was on her knees, the military and police began shooting at the protesters behind her. Eventually, a man was shot
and killed in front of Sister Rose. Sister Rose told the newspaper, "The children ran to the front in embarrassment. "I had no choice
but to pray to God to save them without doing anything."
