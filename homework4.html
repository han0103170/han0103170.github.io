South Korea's first asteroid exploration target, Apophis,
has been spotted near Earth.

Poor access to Earth - 43 times as much distance between the moon.


Enter article 2021.03.11 PM 6:11

The 370-meter-diameter asteroid Apophis approached nearly
16.8 million kilometers from Earth at 10:15 a.m. on the 6th of
this month in Korean time and passed near the Earth at 4.58
kilometers per second. It approached 43 times the distance
between the Earth and the moon, and passed by.

The Korea Astronomy and Space Science Institute succeeded
in observing and releasing the video on the 10th of this
month through tracking and observation using the 'OWL-Net'
from February.

Asteroid Apophis is a celestial body that has been constantly
threatened by Earth's collision since it was first discovered in
2004. It is expected to pass at 6:46 on April 14, 2029 at a
distance of approximately 37,000 km, very close to Earth.

Video capture an asteroid Apophis.

The observational video of Apophis, released by the Korea Astronomy and Space Science Institute, was taken at OWL-Net No. 4 at
the Lemon Mountain Observatory in Arizona.

The Korea Astronomy and Space Science Institute is conducting preliminary research on asteroid exploration missions, especially with
the aim of directly exploring Apophis in 2029. The fact that an asteroid approaches the Earth means that only a small amount of fuel
provides good conditions for the probe to reach the asteroid.
